All the recipes mentioned here are vegan.

Chaat is a term describing savory snacks, typically served at road-side tracks from stalls or food carts in India.The chaat variants are all based on fried dough, with various other ingredients.
The original chaat is a mixture of potato pieces, crisp fried bread dahi vada or dahi bhalla, gram or chickpeas and tangy-salty spices, with sour home-made Indian chilly and saunth (dried ginger and tamarind sauce), fresh green
coriander leaves and yogurt for garnish, but other popular variants included aloo tikkis or samosa (garnished with onion, coriander, hot spices and a dash of curd), bhel puri, dahi puri, panipuri, dahi vada, papri chaat, and sev puri.

Masala Poori

Ingredients:

1 cup peas 3 tablespoon pureed mint
1 small chopped onion 2 medium chopped tomato
1 handful chopped coriander leaves 1/4 cup sev
1 teaspoon salt 2 teaspoon Red chilli powder
1 teaspoon chaat masala powder 2 chopped Mint Leaves
1 handful coriander leaves 2 tablespoon vegetable oil
2 clove 1/2 inch Cinnamon stick
1 teaspoon cumin seeds 3 cloves garlic
1 green chilli 1 large chopped onion
1 chopped carrot 2 mashed,parboiled potato
2 tablespoon Tamrind chutney 12 paani puris

Procedure:

  1. Soak the green peas overnight. Peel the skin of the carrot and potatoes and cut them into cubes. Cook the soaked green peas, carrot and potatoes in a pressure cooker or pan adding water till they become soft.
  2. Heat oil in pan. Add the cumin seeds, cloves, cinnamon and saute for a few seconds. Now add the chopped onions, green chili and garlic cloves and saute till it becomes light brown in color.
  3. Add the boiled and sauteed ingredients along with mint leaves and coriander leaves in a blender and blend it to a smooth paste. Transfer this to a pan. Add little water if required, red chilli powder and chat masala and bring it to a boil.
  4. On a serving plate, crush the puris as desired. Add the prepared masala gravy. (You can also sprinkle some chat masala if desired.) Now add the tamrind chutney and mint chutney (puree) on top of the gravy as per your taste.
  5. Nicely garnish it with chopped onion, tomatoes, coriander leaves and any sev of your choice. The Masala Puri is now ready.
  6. Serve it fresh and hot to enjoy.

Aloo Tikki

Ingredients:

1 cup peeled,mashed,parboiled potato 1/2 cup parboiled,crushed peas
1 tablespoon finely chopped coriander leaves 1 teaspoon chaat masala
1/2 teaspoon spice chili powder 2 tablespoon corn flour
2 tablespoon bread crumbs 1 teaspoon salt
4 tablespoon Refined oil

Procedure:

  1. Combine potatoes, peas, coriander leaves, chaat masala, chilli powder, corn flour, salt in a bowl and mix well.
  2. Divide the mixture into 6 equal portions and shape each portion into a round tikki.
  3. Coat prepared tikkis with breadcrumbs on all sides by rolling it in the breadcrumbs filled plate.
  4. Heat oil in a non-stick tava (griddle) on a medium flame and shallow fry the tikkis, till both sides are golden brown in colour.
  5. Serve hot with tomato ketchup or green chutney.

Dahi Bhalle

Ingredients:

1 cup urad dal 2 pieces green chilli
1 pinch asafoetida 1 teaspoon ginger
1 tablespoon refined oil 4 cup hung curd

For Garnishing

4 teaspoon red chilli powder 4 teaspoon powdered black pepper
4 teaspoon chaat masala 4 teaspoon tamrind chutney
4 teaspoon green chutney

Procedure:

  1. Wash and soak the urad dal in water for 2 hours. Strain the water and reserve for grinding.
  2. Then add it to the blender. Add salt, green chilly, hing and ginger. Add little of the reserved water and grind it to smooth consistency.
  3. The batter should be of dropping consistency and should not be too thick or watery.
  4. Heat oil in a frying pan.Once the oil is hot, wet your hands with water and take a dallop of batter and drop them in hot oil carefully.
  5. Deep fry them until it turns golden brown all over. Strain on a kitchen tissue.
  6. Take warm water in a saucepan. Soak the vadas in the water one by one. Let it rest for 2 mins.Squeeze the water from the vadas gently and take them out.
  7. Your soft and spongy vadas are ready to absorb the yoghurt along with spices.
  8. Mix yoghurt and water together to smooth consistency. Add red chilly powder and salt to taste.Soak the spongy vadas in the yoghurt and allow to rest for 5 to 10 mins.
  9. Transfer it to a serving bowl, and garnish with red chilli powder, pepper powder, chat masala, green chutney and tamarind chutney.
  10. Delicious Dahi Bhalla is ready. Serve immediately.
